On Wednesday (10/26), all four of us met in person at a breakout room in Evans. Given that we had a time crunch, we quickly identified the overall theme of the project and the line items to focus on for the first sprint. We decided to focus on setting up the codebase and infrastructure first, and get a basic structure in place by Sprint 1. The PM for the week (Nithin) curated the most important stories, and we selected the tasks to complete individually for our first sprint.

After the meeting, we continued coordinating through slack messages. Many of us encountered issues with auto deployment as we did not have admin authorization during the first few days of Sprint 1. This meant we initially deployed the codes manually on Heroku. By day 5, we received the admin access and turned on the automatic deploy feature. We were then able to complete all the selected stories in our individual branches and then merge them to the main branch. We now have a structure in place, and will continue to build on this in the next sprint.
