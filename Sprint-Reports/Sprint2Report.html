Sprint-2 description:

Our sprint-2 started on Wednesday (11/2), where all four of us met in person after class. We felt like we could complete more stories this sprint, now that we were familiar with the codebase. So we selected 23 stories (47 story points). One of us pointed out that the workload may be too high, but the PM said that based on early completion of the last sprint, we should overload to better estimate our velocity. Additionally, this would help determine the scope of certain stories we were unsure of before and adjust incomplete stories in the next sprint. This sprint, we significantly underestimated story points for some of our stories - they were much harder than we anticipated. Additionally, one of our developers was unable to complete his story points due to unforeseen personal issues, ending us 14 story points short of the planned 47 (71% completion) at the end of the sprint.

While devs completed work earlier, the PM wanted to walk through the code review process in our meeting on 11/7, causing all story points to be merged to main and marked as complete in a single day. We also had merge conflicts while merging code with the main branch since we had all devs working independently and only planned to merge once all information was complete. To reduce the number of conflicts we had in the future, we decided to create a dev branch to mirror main, where all merging happens at the beginning and end of each individual work session. The intention is that once the development branch functions correctly, then it is merged with Main and deployed. Finally, based on feedback from our initial sprint process, we defined acceptance criteria more closely, assigned GitHub issues to merge points, and closed the relevant issues after raising merge requests to the dev branch.
